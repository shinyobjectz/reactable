# Japanese Edo

## Use when

Use when the visual language should draw from Edo-period Japanese culture, architecture, garments, urban life, or print-era motifs.

## Signature traits

- wood architecture, paper, lacquer, and textile pattern logic
- era-appropriate garments and silhouettes
- merchant, festival, or riverside street atmosphere when relevant
- disciplined ornament rather than indiscriminate 'Japanese' decoration

## Prompt levers

- state whether the focus is street life, festival, interior, bridge scene, theater, or portrait
- use pattern and architecture as structured storytelling elements
- combine with ukiyo-e only when print-language formalism is desired

## Examples

### Example 1 — Festival poster

Edo bridge lantern procession, patterned robes, paper lanterns, wood railings, and flattened decorative flow for title-safe layout.

### Example 2 — Street portrait

Merchant district portrait with fabric texture, shop banners, rain-darkened timber, and disciplined prop choices.

## Avoid

- mixing modern Tokyo signage by accident
- using generic 'Asian' motifs without period specificity
