# Fantasy

## Use when

Use when the scene depends on mythic logic, magical artifacts, legendary creatures, enchanted architecture, or ritual atmosphere.

## Signature traits

- mythic or enchanted world rules
- symbolic props and relics
- material contrast between natural and arcane elements
- heightened atmosphere with clear focal storytelling

## Prompt levers

- decide whether the fantasy is grounded, heroic, dark, folkloric, or luminous
- state how magic becomes visible: light, particles, glyphs, weather, or transformed materials
- anchor one believable environment rule so the magic has something to push against

## Examples

### Example 1 — Relic chamber

Ancient stone vault lit by candlelight and cool moon spill, bronze relic emitting a contained amber aura that kisses nearby dust and carvings.

### Example 2 — Forest procession

Lantern-bearing figures walking through misty cedar groves, protective charms catching warm light, moss and cloth behaving as damp real materials.

## Avoid

- generic purple sparkle soup
- throwing in every fantasy trope without a dominant world logic
