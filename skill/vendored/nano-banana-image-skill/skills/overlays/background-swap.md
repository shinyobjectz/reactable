# Background Swap

## Use when

Use when the subject should remain stable while the environment or backdrop changes.

## Signature traits

- subject preservation
- environment-only replacement
- edge integration and bounce-light awareness

## Prompt levers

- lock crop, pose, geometry, and shadows as needed
- describe the new background material or setting precisely
- state whether the new background should affect color bounce

## Examples

### Example 1 — Packshot swap

Keep the bottle exact, replace matte gray with emerald marble, preserve shadow footprint.

### Example 2 — Portrait swap

Keep subject pose and crop fixed, replace studio backdrop with rainy alley bokeh while preserving face light.

## Avoid

- rewriting wardrobe or pose during the swap
- forgetting that new backgrounds may alter reflected color subtly
