# Surrealism

## Use when

Use when dream logic, symbolic dislocation, or impossible juxtapositions are central to the image's meaning.

## Signature traits

- intentional dream logic
- ordinary spaces disturbed by one or two impossible conditions
- symbolic object relationships
- composed strangeness rather than random weirdness

## Prompt levers

- decide which reality rule bends and which rules remain normal
- keep perspective coherent unless warped space is the point
- let impossible elements cast real light and shadow when useful

## Examples

### Example 1 — Museum dream

Orderly atrium with impossible floating koi and a staircase folding back into itself while maintaining a believable camera view.

### Example 2 — Desert clock tableau

Sparse desert plateau, elongated dusk shadows, and one large melting clock draped over a stone monolith.

## Avoid

- random symbol piles with no compositional logic
- breaking every physical rule at once
