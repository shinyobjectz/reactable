# iPhone Night Mode

## Use when

Use when the image should feel like a flagship-phone night capture with computational dynamic range, controlled shadow lift, and realistic handheld intimacy.

## Signature traits

- smartphone-sized viewpoint intimacy
- lifted shadows without losing night feel
- localized sharpening rather than cinema-lens softness
- available-light realism with subtle computational polish

## Prompt levers

- keep lens feel wide-to-normal rather than telephoto glamour unless specified
- preserve believable noise and highlight control
- avoid turning it into full studio perfection

## Examples

### Example 1 — Street portrait

Close portrait under rain-wet market awnings with soft shadow lift and practical highlights preserved.

### Example 2 — Night café scene

Available-light table scene with readable subject, controlled warm bulbs, and lifted but not flattened dark areas.

## Avoid

- cinema-camera bokeh fantasy unless the user wants a hybrid look
- extreme CG crispness
