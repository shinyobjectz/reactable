# Drone

## Use when

Use when the camera should feel airborne, elevated, surveying, or gliding through space.

## Signature traits

- elevated or hovering perspective
- clear parallax and scale layers
- broad environmental storytelling
- flight-camera movement potential

## Prompt levers

- state altitude or relative height implicitly through visible layers
- use roads, rooftops, water, or crowds to show scale
- keep perspective consistent with a flying camera

## Examples

### Example 1 — Establishing city frame

Low-hover drone sweeping over neon service alleys with rooftop clutter and atmospheric depth.

### Example 2 — Festival overhead

Lantern river procession viewed from a graceful descending angle with layered crowd geometry.

## Avoid

- eye-level composition with a 'drone' label slapped on top
- telephoto compression that erases the airborne feeling
