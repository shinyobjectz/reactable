
# Creative Brief

## Purpose

Translate a marketing, editorial, or storytelling objective into visual direction.

## Brief fields

- objective
- audience
- emotional outcome
- proof point or message
- profile / platform
- visual constraints
- brand tone

## Example 1 — luxury bottle campaign

Objective: make the bottle feel premium and gift-worthy  
Audience: design-conscious skincare buyers  
Outcome: calm confidence, not loud glamour  
Visual response: restrained palette, tactile finish, elegant spacing, controlled reflections

## Example 2 — science classroom infographic

Objective: explain exoplanet classes clearly  
Audience: middle school students and teachers  
Outcome: curiosity plus comprehension  
Visual response: large labels, simple panel logic, friendly but not childish palette, clean iconography

## Example 3 — YouTube vlog cover

Objective: make the host feel approachable and high-energy  
Audience: casual lifestyle audience  
Outcome: immediate click interest  
Visual response: face-forward crop, readable title zone, bright but limited accent colors, background simplified for thumbnail legibility

## Translation move

Turn abstract business goals into visible decisions.

### Before
"Make it premium and engaging."

### After
"Use sparse composition, one jewel accent color, polished highlights with controlled falloff, and a centered subject with clean title-safe margins."

That is how strategy becomes pixels instead of executive mist.
