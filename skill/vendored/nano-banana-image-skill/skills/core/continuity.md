
# Continuity

## Purpose

Keep characters, products, or environments consistent across multiple outputs.

## Continuity sheet

Create a compact continuity sheet with:
- locked silhouette traits,
- locked colors and materials,
- locked costume or product geometry,
- allowed variables,
- forbidden drift points.

## Example 1 — character series

Locked:
- helmet silhouette
- orange visor tint
- chest emblem placement
- white suit panel seams

Allowed to vary:
- weather
- time of day
- camera distance
- environment mood

## Example 2 — product campaign

Locked:
- bottle geometry
- label proportions
- cap material
- brand colors

Allowed:
- background
- lighting mood
- prop accents
- camera elevation within a small range

## Example 3 — episodic poster set

Locked:
- title treatment
- margin system
- logo block
- protagonist costume silhouette

Allowed:
- episode color theme
- supporting motifs
- weather and atmosphere

## Continuity moves

- reference a hero frame,
- keep a short lock list,
- specify what may vary,
- reuse the same aspect ratio unless a platform change forces otherwise.

## Failure modes

- asking for "same character" without identifying stable traits,
- varying camera angle so much that the subject feels different,
- changing lighting so aggressively that materials look new,
- swapping palettes without protecting brand colors.

## Best practice

Continuity is easier when you write:
- **keep these exact**
- **vary only these**
- **do not introduce**
