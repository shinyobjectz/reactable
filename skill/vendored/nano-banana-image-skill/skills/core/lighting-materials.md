
# Lighting & Materials

## Purpose

Tell the model how light behaves and how surfaces respond.

## Lighting stack

- key light
- fill behavior
- rim or edge light
- practicals
- atmosphere
- color temperature relationship

## Material stack

- primary material
- finish
- specularity
- translucency or opacity
- wear or cleanliness
- moisture, dust, or age

## Example 1 — wet neon portrait

- key: warm stall practical from camera right
- fill: cool cyan ambient bounce
- rim: thin magenta edge
- atmosphere: fine rain mist
- materials: wet nylon, damp skin, reflective metal zipper

## Example 2 — clean product shot

- key: large soft top-front source
- fill: restrained frontal return
- rim: very subtle back edge
- atmosphere: none
- materials: frosted glass bottle, satin label, brushed aluminum cap

## Example 3 — storm twilight relight

- key: dim cool sky source
- fill: low ambient blue-gray
- rim: sharp cyan from back right
- atmosphere: distant rain curtain and thin fog
- materials: soaked pavement, dark stone, slick cloth edges

## Rules

- Light and material must agree.
- Fog is only visible when lit.
- Highly polished surfaces need controlled highlight logic.
- Matte surfaces do not suddenly sparkle like lacquer.

## Common failures

- generic "dramatic lighting" with no structure,
- materials not responding to the stated light,
- overusing rim light until everything glows like a nightclub shrimp.
