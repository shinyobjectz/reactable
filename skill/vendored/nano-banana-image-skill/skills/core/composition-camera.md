
# Composition & Camera

## Purpose

Specify what the camera sees, from where, and with what spatial emphasis.

## Required decisions

- shot type
- framing
- camera angle
- lens feel
- depth behavior
- aspect ratio
- negative space

## Example 1 — editorial portrait

- shot: waist-up
- angle: slightly low but intimate
- lens feel: 50mm
- depth: shallow but readable
- aspect ratio: 4:5
- negative space: upper-left title-safe pocket

## Example 2 — drone establishing frame

- shot: elevated wide
- angle: shallow descending perspective
- lens feel: 24mm
- depth: broad readable layers with atmospheric haze
- aspect ratio: 16:9

## Example 3 — product hero

- shot: centered tabletop three-quarter
- angle: slight eye-level to product shoulder
- lens feel: 80–100mm
- depth: crisp focus on label plane, soft falloff behind

## Camera language cheatsheet

### Intimate
- 50mm to 85mm
- stable framing
- cleaner backgrounds
- closer face area

### Documentary
- 28mm to 50mm
- believable handheld or observational placement
- less glamourized symmetry

### Epic / environmental
- 18mm to 35mm
- layered foreground-midground-background
- stronger parallax and scale

## Failure modes

- vague "cinematic angle" with no actual framing,
- impossible lens behavior,
- no negative space despite poster text needs,
- calling for a drone view but describing eye-level composition.

## Quick fix

If the image feels directionless, add:
- one explicit shot type,
- one camera angle,
- one lens feel,
- one statement about negative space.
