
# World Model

## Purpose

Ensure the image behaves as if it belongs to a coherent world.

A prompt needs a world model when:
- multiple objects interact,
- historical or cultural details matter,
- the camera moves through space,
- materials react to weather or light,
- surreal elements must still feel intentional.

## Questions to answer

- What are the physical rules?
- What era or technology level exists here?
- What materials are present?
- What is the dominant light environment?
- What does the camera actually see from this position?

## Example 1 — sci-fi alley

World model:
- cramped service alley between mid-rise commercial buildings,
- bilingual LED signage and wet metal shutters,
- overhead cable clutter,
- vapor haze revealing depth,
- flying camera at shoulder-to-second-story height.

Without that model, the alley becomes a glowing soup.

## Example 2 — fantasy relic table

World model:
- hand-forged brass,
- worn oak,
- candlelight plus cool moon spill,
- dust disturbed around a recently placed relic,
- parchment edges curled by age.

Now the props share a reality instead of feeling dropped from different stock photos.

## Example 3 — surreal museum

World model:
- normal museum architecture and perspective,
- impossible fish movement through air,
- shadows remain coherent,
- glass reflections obey the same camera and light rules.

The impossible part should be chosen, not contagious.

## Common failures

- impossible camera sightlines,
- fog visible with no light to reveal it,
- polished chrome in a matte lighting setup,
- characters holding objects at the wrong scale,
- historical costumes mixed with modern fastening logic accidentally.

## Repair move

Add one sentence explaining how the environment works.

Example:
"Rain has just stopped, leaving reflective pavement and damp cloth surfaces that catch warm storefront light and cool overhead ambient spill."
