
# Grounding

## Purpose

Make the image obey reality, or intentionally depart from reality in a controlled way.

Grounding means:
- correct materials,
- plausible architecture,
- coherent era details,
- sensible props,
- and visual logic that does not wobble like a wet cardboard throne.

## Ground first, stylize second

Always decide:
- **where** the scene exists,
- **when** it exists,
- **what objects belong there**,
- **how light behaves there**,
- **what cultural or historical details are essential**.

Then apply style.

## What to ground explicitly

### Subject grounding
- anatomy and silhouette,
- attire appropriate to era or world,
- gear, tools, and props.

### Scene grounding
- architecture,
- vegetation,
- signage,
- weather effects,
- street furniture,
- interior materials.

### Cultural grounding
- motifs,
- ornament logic,
- textiles,
- calligraphy style,
- social context,
- visual symbols.

## Example 1 — historical scene

**Weak:**  
"A Mughal miniature of a royal court, beautiful colors."

**Grounded:**  
"Mughal miniature court scene with jewel-toned textiles, pietra dura inlay, low seated courtiers on patterned carpets, profile-leaning poses, fine gold detailing, scalloped arches, and garden geometry appropriate to imperial Mughal architecture."

The second version has bones.

## Example 2 — urban night edit

**Weak:**  
"Night market with lots of detail."

**Grounded:**  
"Covered night market alley with damp concrete, bilingual vendor signs, fluorescent practicals, reflective tarps, hanging produce crates, narrow passage depth, and puddle reflections that respond to overhead light sources."

## Example 3 — surrealism with structure

Surreal scenes still need internal logic.

**Bad surreal:**  
"Random floating fish, clocks, stairs, galaxy fog, and cathedral windows."

**Better surreal:**  
"Dreamlike museum atrium where oversized koi drift through air as if swimming in invisible water, long shadow-casting clocks melt over balustrades, and a single impossible staircase folds back into itself while preserving coherent perspective from the camera position."

The dream can be strange. It should not be lazy.

## Grounding prompts by category

### Architecture
- era,
- region,
- load-bearing style,
- surface wear,
- glazing, stone, brick, wood, or metal behavior.

### Product
- manufacturing finish,
- label placement,
- seam lines,
- molded edges,
- packaging logic.

### Portrait
- skin behavior,
- fabric drape,
- hair response to humidity or wind,
- accessory wear.

## Anti-patterns

- medieval armor with modern tactical seams unless the hybrid is deliberate,
- Edo streets with electric billboards unless the prompt frames a time blend,
- marble behaving like plastic under light,
- fog added where no light source can reveal it.

## Quick repair move

When a prompt feels generic, add one sentence that anchors:
- era,
- material,
- use-case,
- and environmental logic.

That one sentence often upgrades the whole image.
