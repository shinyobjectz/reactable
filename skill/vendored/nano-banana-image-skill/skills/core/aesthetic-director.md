
# Aesthetic Director

## Purpose

Provide the taste layer that turns a technically correct prompt into something intentional.

Aesthetic direction should affect:
- restraint versus excess,
- elegance versus chaos,
- softness versus graphic contrast,
- intimacy versus spectacle,
- sincerity versus irony.

## Director questions

- What emotional temperature should the image have?
- Should it feel polished, raw, playful, severe, ecstatic, restrained?
- What should the eye notice first?
- What should remain quiet?

## Example 1 — restrained luxury

Direction:
- sparse composition,
- disciplined reflections,
- dark neutrals with one jewel accent,
- expensive materials shown calmly, not shouted.

## Example 2 — feverish festival energy

Direction:
- rhythmic crowd motion,
- layered warm lantern light,
- compressed depth,
- decorative abundance without obliterating the focal path.

## Example 3 — gentle scientific infographic

Direction:
- calm spacing,
- legible hierarchy,
- soft but trustworthy palette,
- clean iconography,
- zero carnival nonsense.

## Art direction levers

- contrast ratio
- palette intensity
- pattern density
- edge sharpness
- atmosphere density
- camera intimacy
- negative space discipline

## Anti-pattern

Do not replace concrete decisions with mood poetry alone.

"melancholic but triumphant and dreamlike" is fine as a tone note,
but it does not tell the camera or the layout what to do.
