# Portrait Base

## Use when

Use for people-first imagery where expression, identity, pose, wardrobe, and face-area hierarchy matter most.

## Signature traits

- identity and expression are the focal engine
- camera distance and lens feel strongly shape intimacy
- wardrobe and background support the face rather than compete with it

## Prompt levers

- decide face area, crop, and lens feel first
- protect identity locks in edits
- control background clutter aggressively

## Examples

### Example 1 — Editorial night portrait

Waist-up crop, 50mm feel, cool-warm mixed practicals, wet jacket texture, controlled negative space for cover lines.

### Example 2 — Studio portrait

Clean key/fill structure, neutral backdrop, expression-led focus, restrained wardrobe detail.

## Avoid

- letting scenic background details overpower the face
- rewriting identity when the user asked for a lighting or outfit change
